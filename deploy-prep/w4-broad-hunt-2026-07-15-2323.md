## Found, not fixed (below the "clearly live-exploitable" bar)

**`apply-ceo/route.ts` line 111**: the "applicant confirmation" email
interpolates `name.split(' ')[0]` into HTML unescaped
(`<h2>...${name.split(' ')[0]}!</h2>`), while the sibling `contact/route.ts`
and `inquiry/route.ts` consistently wrap every user-supplied field in
`escapeHtml()`. This is HTML injection into an email — but the email is
sent only to the address the same submitter supplied (`to: email`), so it's
self-XSS against the attacker's own inbox, not a vector against anyone
else. Flagging as an inconsistency worth a one-line fix for defense-in-depth
consistency, not fixing unilaterally since it's not exploitable against a
third party.
